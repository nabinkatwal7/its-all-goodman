Walter Hartwell White was born in 1958 in Albuquerque, New Mexico. A gifted chemist, he co-founded Gray Matter Technologies with Elliott Schwartz and Gretchen Schwartz — but left the company early, selling his shares for $5,000, a decision that would haunt him for decades.

After leaving Gray Matter, Walt became a high school chemistry teacher, living a quiet suburban life with his wife Skyler and son Walter Jr. Everything changed in 2008 when he was diagnosed with terminal lung cancer.

Facing medical bills and the desire to secure his family's future, Walt partnered with former student Jesse Pinkman to manufacture and sell crystal methamphetamine. What began as a desperate act evolved into something far darker — the birth of **Heisenberg**, a persona defined by green pork pie hats, 99.1% pure blue meth, and an empire built on violence, manipulation, and pride.

From cooking in an RV in the desert to running a state-of-the-art superlab under Gus Fring's laundry, Walt's transformation is the moral core of the Breaking Bad universe. He didn't just break bad — he became the danger himself.
